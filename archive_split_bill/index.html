💡 1. Design Solution

Envisioned design solution addressing the split-bill brief within physical KTV singing environments.

🎯 Problem Context & Brief Standard group bill splitting creates intense cognitive friction in loud KTV party settings. Users must switch between chat lists, calculation tools, and banks.
✨ Guided KTV-Specific Workflow We integrated smart receipt scanning, member matching, and **branching split actions** (Equal vs. Custom) within a highly intuitive dark frosted UI.

📋 2. Heuristic Evaluation

Summary of major usability friction points identified through heuristic review and responsive design revisions.

Heuristic & Issue Design Response / UI Change
Visibility of system status
Users felt unsure if a selection registered. 		Added interactive chips, active highlights, and a dynamic status indicator.
Match with real world
System labels used banking terms instead of party words. 		Adopted natural payment phrases like "Split Bill", "Items", and "Confirm".
Error prevention
Accidental early confirmation caused payment errors. 		Implemented a detailed Verify Checklist screen before committing to real bank transactions.
Recognition over recall
Users had to remember who ordered what. 		Embedded dynamic avatar initial chips directly in individual receipt item lines.
🔄 3. Iterations with Annotations

Evolution of screen layouts showing responses to feedback received.

Round 1: Process Separation Usability
Problem: Early draft loaded setup, members, scanning, and totals onto a single cluttered screen.
Change: Segmented flow into a clear 4-stage guided sub-process.
Outcome: User task completion time improved, reducing mental load.
Round 2: Flexible Path Choice Efficiency
Problem: Standard auto-split forced equal sharing, causing tension when spending differed.
Change: Added clear custom branching buttons (Equal vs. Custom) on the Bill Overview screen.
Outcome: Enabled rapid auto-shares alongside deep customizable allocations.
Round 3: Final Security Gate Validation
Problem: Initiators lacked a reassuring step before launching real-time payment notifications.
Change: Designed the Confirm & Pay checklist page containing triple-point verification blocks.
Outcome: User confidence ratings raised significantly.
🎨 Cohesive Color Palette Sync Retained visual continuity by employing desaturated Morandi tones derived from our original search song interface to create a sophisticated, desaturated KTV digital ecosystem.
⭐ 4. Key Visual Checkpoints

How feedback from evaluation sessions shaped key features in our high-fidelity design.

1 Frictionless Initiation One distinct Split Bill trigger button on Room Dashboard launches the payment flow, retaining active group details in background cache automatically.
2 Group Cohort Builder Active participants are mapped instantly. The initiator can add or remove members with single-tap selections to minimize error rates.
3 Distinct Action Branching The receipt overview shows totals and branching actions: Custom Split directs to item selection while Auto Calculation resolves instantly.
4 Real-time Payment Tracker Post-checkout, the initiator views a live tracker card indicating each member's settlement status to replace direct social pressure.

🔗 B. Previously Designed Search Flow & Visual Anchors

Fusing Pre-existing UI Foundations with New Workflows

To maintain visual continuity throughout the design assignment, our new 9-page Split Bill subsystem builds directly upon the pre-existing mobile search flow designed in previous iterations.

Integrated Aspects:

  • Color Palette Synthesis: Extracted the exact 10 colors from the previous dot configuration (shown in the image margin) ranging from warm rose gradients to deep royal blues.
  • Typography & Card Curvature: Preserved the standard modern curvature and Inter font configurations established in the active "Search Songs" dashboard views.

📝 C. Heuristic Reflections & Verification

Our KTV design directly implements Nielsen Norman's interaction principles to eliminate typical mobile checkout anxiety:

  • Flexibility & Efficiency: The branching decision points on Page 4 enable rapid equal splits for simple groups, while keeping customizable shares accessible for advanced users.
  • Consistency & Standards: Icons, transaction protocols, and digital wallet selectors adhere strictly to established iOS 16 Pro Max guidelines.
  • Help & Documentation: Dynamic checklists on Page 7 verify balances prior to debit actions, acting as real-time, context-aware user guidance.
🧠 D. Iterative Logic & Design Evolution

Development shifted focus from standard raw math capabilities to deep social-emotional considerations within KTV gathering situations:

  • Emotional Comfort: Automating group notifications and reminders replaces direct text messaging, mitigating social pressure.
  • Progressive Disclosure: Masking detailed custom fields until specifically triggered on Page 5 keeps standard flows clean and accessible.
  • Zero-State Fail-safes: If friends forget their devices, initiators can easily assign shares to custom offline names.
