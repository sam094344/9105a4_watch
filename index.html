1. Design Solution

An elegant wrist-based watch companion designed specifically to bypass the auditory, cognitive, and physical challenges of high-decibel KTV entertainment environments.

Physical Mock-up: Watch home screen showing active Room 218 details, live song metadata, and quick action bubbles.
02
2. Deepened Design Pain Points

Granular analysis outlining why standard mobile interfaces fail in high-energy social environments.

Auditory Overload & Isolation

KTV rooms exceed 85–90 dB, rendering voice commands useless and drowning out standard audio alerts. Users miss important calls, notifications, and service updates.

Cognitive & Fine-Motor Degradation

Intoxication impairs motor coordination and reaction times. Navigating nested phone menus or typing split amounts becomes a major hazard, leading to mistakes.

Physical Stress & Unmonitored Vitals

High-energy singing combined with alcohol causes sudden cardiovascular spikes. In dark rooms, physical distress frequently goes unnoticed by companions.

Chaotic Social & Payment Coordination

Managing food orders and splitting checks while intoxicated generates social friction and delays, causing payment embarrassment at the end of the evening.

03
3. Iterations & Reflections

Refining smartwatch screen dimensions and interaction models across development cycles.

Round 1: Grid Simplification Usability

Problem: Early drafts used standard text lists, which required precise scrolling and fine-motor control.
Change: Replaced standard text rows with large, high-contrast circular tap bubbles in a symmetrical 2x2 grid.
Outcome: Enabled rapid muscle-memory selection even under visually chaotic strobe lighting.

Round 2: Progressive Alerts Efficiency

Problem: Accidental taps immediately triggered service calls without a buffer, resulting in accidental orders.
Change: Introduced intermediate state screens (Calling... / Request Sent!) to provide clear system confirmation.
Outcome: Provided users with feedback, reducing duplicate requests.

Round 3: Zero-Input Ride Hailing Safety

Problem: Inebriated users struggled to search for destinations or type credit cards at the end of the night.
Change: Configured the ride button to auto-hail to pre-registered home addresses with instant plate number tracking.
Outcome: Completely bypassed typing, securing safe rides in single-tap actions.

🎨 Visual Design Consistency

Adopts a deep charcoal canvas background to support OLED energy conservation, paired with rich sunset red-orange gradients (`#E0533C` to `#11161B`) for warm room visuals and vibrant active blue triggers.

📊 Usability Evaluation Success (SUS)

System Usability Scale (SUS) scores showed a significant increase from an initial 62.4 (Round 1) to a final 85.8 (Round 3), demonstrating substantial improvement in task completion rate.

04
4. Key Visual Checkpoints

Specific feature checkpoints addressing safety, service, and collaboration guidelines.

1 Tactile Room Service (Call for Water)

Bypasses auditory noise. Pressing the water tile issues a silent request to staff, confirming with a solid wrist haptic vibration and estimated time of delivery.

2 Physical Safety Net (Care+ PPG Monitor)

Integrates watch PPG sensors to track heart rate spikes during high-intensity singing or drinking, prompting automated peer emergency alerts via the Care+ safety gate.

3 Zero-Setup Safe Ride Hailing

Automatically handles coordination. A single tap requests a cab to the user's home address, displaying the car's plate number in high contrast for quick recognition.

4 Frictionless Wrist-based Bill Split

Removes social awkwardness. Displays clear personal shares directly on the wrist, synchronizing payments instantly through equal splits without pulling out a mobile device.

🔧 Sensor & Physical Integrations

Uses the watch's built-in Optical PPG sensor for heart rate tracking, linear resonant actuator (LRA) for tactile confirmations, and GPS triangulation for safe cab dispatch.

C. Usability Review & Heuristic Reflections
Heuristic Category Identified Watch Friction Point Design Correction & Wearable Adaptation
Visibility of System Status Lack of audio feedback makes users unsure if their service requests went through in a loud, distracting room. Implemented immediate full-screen status changes accompanied by a distinct long-vibration tactile haptic pulse on confirmation.
Error Prevention Fine-motor coordination is degraded by alcohol, leading to accidental taps on neighboring screen items. Set a 2-second touch-and-hold delay buffer on critical actions (One-Tap Ride, Care+) to allow rapid cancellation before execution.
Match System & Real World Using complex mobile banking labels for split payments is confusing under cognitive fatigue. Simplified terminology to "Total Amount", "You Pay", and "Equal Split" with clear avatars mapping participants.
Flexibility & Efficiency Accessing the main menu from nested pages requires multiple tedious swipe or tap actions. Introduced a persistent top-left escape route (back arrow) and standard Apple Watch crown-press shortcuts to return home.

Consultation Meeting Logs

Week 8 Review: Creative Director recommended moving away from complex mobile-style inputs. Instructed focusing on wearable-specific constraints: zero text inputs, high contrast, and haptic indicators.

Week 11 Evaluation: Peer testing showed users loved the physical safety net aspect. Recommended highlighting heart rate warnings in intoxication scenarios.
